Q17 – “…when they don’t think they’re making enough progress…give up hope…stop believing…” - these phrases are all about losing confidence or motivation. - Correct answer: B - loss of confidence Q18 – “…takes a lot of determination and hard work…lots of people don’t have that kind of commitment.” - this is about the effort and long-term dedication needed. - Correct answer C - too much effort required. 💡 Why You Missed It:

  • You swapped the ideas: You took “hard work” and applied it to Q17, but that part comes second.

  • In IELTS MCQs, the order of the ideas usually matches the order of the questions.

  • You may have guessed based on vocabulary, not on exact function and meaning.

1.3.1 Common Paraphrases for Multiple-Choice Options

🅰️ A. Lack of Time

🔑 Key Concept: Not enough time available to do something regularly

🟢 Common Paraphrases in Listening: - “Don’t have time to…” - “Too busy with work/studies” - “Not enough hours in the day” - “Schedule is too full” - “No free time / hardly any time” - “It’s hard to fit it into my routine”

🅱️ B. Loss of Confidence

🔑 Key Concept: Losing belief in your ability to succeed

🟢 Common Paraphrases: - “Don’t believe they can do it anymore” - “Gave up hope” - “Think they’ll never succeed” - “Felt like a failure” - “Didn’t trust themselves” - “Started to doubt whether it was possible”

1.3.2 🅲 C. Too Much Effort Required

🔑 Key Concept: It’s too hard or tiring to continue

🟢 Common Paraphrases: - “Takes a lot of hard work” - “Physically demanding / exhausting” - “You have to push yourself every day” - “It’s tough to keep going” - “Requires a lot of determination and discipline” - “People underestimate how much energy it takes”

1.3.3 🅳 D. High Costs

🔑 Key Concept: Too expensive to afford

🟢 Common Paraphrases: - “It’s quite pricey” - “Costs too much money” - “Couldn’t afford it” - “Membership fees are high” - “Didn’t have the budget for it” - “Financially, it wasn’t sustainable”

1.3.4 🅴 E. Feeling Less Successful Than Others

🔑 Key Concept: Comparing yourself to others and feeling inadequate

🟢 Common Paraphrases: - “Others were doing better” - “Felt like I was falling behind” - “Everyone else seemed more advanced” - “Compared myself to others” - “Didn’t progress as quickly as my peers” - “I looked around and saw I wasn’t improving like them”

1.3.5 🔁 Tip for Listening

When you’re preparing for multiple-choice questions:

  • Underline keywords in the options.
  • Anticipate the type of language or emotion they might use.
  • Remember they often paraphrase the idea—not the exact words.
