6 Crime

Word Backslide (verb)
Definition To return to a worse condition or a previous bad habit after making progress or improvement
Part of Speech Verb (irregular: backslide ‚Äì backslid ‚Äì backslid/backslidden)
Common IELTS Themes Environment, Society, Health, Education, Crime
IELTS-Level Synonyms regress, relapse, deteriorate, decline
Example Sentences - Despite years of progress, the country has recently begun to backslide in its efforts to reduce pollution.
- Some argue that strict policies are needed to prevent society from backsliding into inequality and unrest.
- Patients who lack support are more likely to backslide into unhealthy eating habits.
Band 7 - 9 Usage - Use ‚Äúbackslide‚Äù in Task 2 essays when talking about setbacks in areas such as education reform, social equality, or environmental protection.
- Best used in formal writing when describing negative trends or reversal of progress.

Use this word especially when writing about policies that have failed, declining social values, or countries returning to outdated practices. It adds a nuanced, high-level tone to your writing.
